Write a draft proposal of a post-conflict needs assessment memo. Justify your recommendations using evidence from cases.

Full Instructions:

After reading the background material, please write a draft proposal of a post-conflict needs assessment memo based on the information you have at your disposal. Be sure to include the following elements:

  1. Conflict and Needs Assessment (List, in order of risk, current threats to the country’s stability and challenges to its development.)
  2. Recommended Policy Responses
  3. Recommended Programming Options

Please divide your policy and programming recommendations into two sections, distinguishing between recommendations for government of Nimpala and recommendations for the international donor community.

You may also want to distinguish between short-term “quick wins” versus longer-term responses. Please prioritize your recommendations with the understanding that the amount of aid to Nimpala has not yet been guaranteed or dispersed and available resources are quite limited.

Justify your recommendations using evidence from cases. Such cases can be drawn from your professional experiences and/or class readings.

These should be between 3 and 6 pages.

Initial Questions:
  1. What information will you need to perform a post-conflict needs assessment?
  2. How will you go about collecting that information? What challenges do you expect to encounter?
  3. What role has the environment and natural resources played in this conflict? To what extent should environmental needs be prioritized in the context of Nimpala’s political and economic challenges?
  4. Who are the “dividers” and “connectors” in the conflict? Are there any potential spoilers.
  5. Based on the initial background discussions, what specific studies or information would you need? From trade reports to climate change predictions to specific population movement?
  6. What limitations are there for collecting additional information? What strategy do you have for engaging the national government? What other partners would you approach (Universities, NGOs)
